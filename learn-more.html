Climate change

Scientific evidence for warming of the climate system is unequivocal. - Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change

Climate change effects animals, agriculture and the human population. Over time climate change will reshape the face of our planet and while some amount of climate change is natural, there's a more than 95 percent probability that human activities over the past 50 years have warmed our planet. Currently, climate change has caused glaciers to shrink, ice on rivers and lakes to break up earlier, plant and animal ranges to shift and trees to flower sooner. CO2 Emissions

Gases that trap heat in the atmosphere are called greenhouse gases. CO2 is the secound most abundent greenhouse gas behind water vapor. Carbon dioxide enters the atmosphere through burning fossil fuels like coal, natural gas, and oil. It also enters the atmosphere as a result of certain chemical reactions. Greenhouse gases cause the Earth to warm, which will eventually change our climate altoghether. A changing climate has a range of potential ecological, physical and health impacts, including extreme weather events, sea-level rise, altered crop growth, and disrupted water systems.

Pro-tip: One way you can cut down on your CO2 emissions is to buy energy and water efficient appliances!
