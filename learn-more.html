Climate change

Scientific evidence for warming of the climate system is unequivocal. - Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change

Climate change effects animals, agriculture and the human population. Over time climate change will reshape the face of our planet and while some amount of climate change is natural, there's a more than 95 percent probability that human activities over the past 50 years have warmed our planet. Currently, climate change has caused glaciers to shrink, ice on rivers and lakes to break up earlier, plant and animal ranges to shift and trees to flower sooner. For more information on the evidence, causes, effects and solutions of global climate change visit NASA: Climate Change

CO2 Emissions

Gases that trap heat in the atmosphere are called greenhouse gases. CO2 is the secound most abundent greenhouse gas behind water vapor. Carbon dioxide enters the atmosphere through burning fossil fuels like coal, natural gas, and oil. It also enters the atmosphere as a result of certain chemical reactions. Greenhouse gases cause the Earth to warm, which will eventually change our climate altoghether. A changing climate has a range of potential ecological, physical and health impacts, including extreme weather events, sea-level rise, altered crop growth, and disrupted water systems.

Pro-tip: One way you can cut down on your CO2 emissions is to buy energy and water efficient appliances!

Ocean Pollution

Ocean pollution is the spreading of harmful substances such as oil, plastic, industrial and agricultural waste and chemical particles into the ocean. This waste has caused devastating effects on marine life. It is the responsibility of every citizen to play his or her part in making these oceans clean so that marine species can thrive for a long period of time. Most of the debris in the ocean does not decompose and remain in the ocean for years. These debries cause oxygen levels to go down as they decompose. Low oxygen levels are dangerous to marine life. Some other dangers to our ocean include: sewage, toxic chemicals from industries, land runoff, large scale oil spills, and ocean mining.

Pro-tip: You can help save the ocean by cutting down on single use plastics!

Check out the Ocean Conservancy to learn more on how to help more.

Electronic Pollution

As technology becomes more critical in our environment, so has its demand. Technology corporations have created a massive growth in electronic waste by making sure their products last a few years before creating new products to keep up with the industry. The high cost of repairing the product also tends to spark more replacing the old with the new one at a cheaper rate. Electronics contain valuable elements like gold and silver, yet they also contain toxic materials as well. Many of the materials and elements in electronics can be recycled and help prevent the destruction of many animal habitats. Recycling these parts may help reduce the 20 to 50 million metric tons of e-waste that are disposed of worldwide every year.

Pro-tip: Is that extra gadget really necessary? Try finding one device with multiple functions to cut down on e-waste!
